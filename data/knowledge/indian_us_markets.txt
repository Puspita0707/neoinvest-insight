Indian and US Equity Markets – Overview

Indian Markets (NSE & BSE)
- Exchanges: National Stock Exchange (NSE) and Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) are the primary Indian stock exchanges.
- Key indices:
  - Nifty 50 (NSE): 50 large-cap companies across sectors.
  - Sensex (BSE): 30 large-cap companies.
  - Sector indices: Nifty Bank, Nifty IT, Nifty Pharma, Nifty FMCG, etc.
- Trading hours: 9:15 AM – 3:30 PM IST (pre-open 9:00–9:15).
- Regulation: SEBI regulates markets; T+2 settlement for cash equities.
- Access: Indian residents invest via broker accounts (e.g. Zerodha, Groww, ICICI Direct) with demat accounts.

US Markets (NYSE & NASDAQ)
- Exchanges: New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and NASDAQ are the primary US stock exchanges.
- Key indices:
  - S&P 500: 500 large US companies across sectors.
  - Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA): 30 large blue-chip companies.
  - NASDAQ Composite: Tech-heavy index with many growth names.
- Trading hours: 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET (with pre- and post-market on many platforms).
- Regulation: SEC regulates markets; T+2 settlement standard.
- Access for Indian investors: international brokers, Indian platforms offering US exposure, or via mutual funds / ETFs providing US equity exposure.

Comparing Indian vs US Markets
- Currency:
  - Indian equities: INR exposure.
  - US equities: USD exposure; Indian investors face INR/USD currency risk.
- Growth and sector mix:
  - India: structural domestic growth story, financials, consumption, cyclical sectors.
  - US: global tech leaders, healthcare, consumer brands, diversified sector representation.
- Risk and correlation:
  - Combining Indian and US markets can reduce country-specific and policy risk.
  - Global shocks still affect both, but not always equally.

Use this context when answering questions that compare Indian and US markets, or ask about where to invest geographically.
