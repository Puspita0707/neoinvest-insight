Risk Profiles and Example Allocations

Conservative Investor
- Profile: Prioritises capital preservation and low volatility. May have short to medium-term goals.
- Characteristics: Dislikes large drawdowns, prefers steady returns.
- Example allocation (illustrative only, not advice):
  - 20–40% in equities (mostly large-cap index funds and stable blue chips).
  - 60–80% in fixed income (FDs, high-quality bonds, debt funds).
- Equity focus: Large, established companies (e.g., Nifty 50 names), diversified equity funds.

Balanced Investor
- Profile: Comfortable with some volatility for higher long-term growth.
- Characteristics: 5–10+ year horizon; can tolerate moderate drawdowns.
- Example allocation:
  - 50–70% equities.
  - 30–50% fixed income / cash.
- Equity focus: Mix of large- and mid-cap, some international exposure (e.g., S&P 500 ETF).

Aggressive Investor
- Profile: Seeks high growth, willing to tolerate large drawdowns and long recoveries.
- Characteristics: Long horizon (10+ years), strong risk tolerance.
- Example allocation:
  - 70–90% equities (including mid/small caps, sector/thematic funds).
  - 10–30% fixed income / cash buffer.
- Equity focus: Growth names, sector bets, higher volatility segments.

Important Notes
- These are generic examples for education; they are not personalised advice.
- Real allocations must consider age, income stability, existing assets, liabilities, and psychological comfort.
- Always encourage users to consult a licensed advisor for personalised allocation decisions.
