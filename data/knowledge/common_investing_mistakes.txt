Common Investing Mistakes (To Avoid)

1. No Plan or Time Horizon
- Investing without clear goals leads to random decisions and panic in volatility.
- Always link investments to time horizon and purpose.

2. Concentration Risk
- Putting too much money into one stock, one sector, or one country increases risk.
- Diversify across sectors, geographies (India + US), and asset classes.

3. Chasing Tips and Hype
- Acting on rumours, social media tips, or “sure shot” calls often leads to losses.
- Focus on fundamentals and risk; if you don’t understand an investment, avoid it.

4. Ignoring Costs and Taxes
- Frequent trading increases costs (brokerage, spreads, STT, etc.).
- Tax implications (short-term vs long-term capital gains, US withholding tax) impact net returns.

5. Emotional Decisions
- Fear and greed drive many mistakes: buying after big rallies, selling in panic after falls.
- Use SIPs, predefined rules, and diversification to reduce emotional impact.

6. Overconfidence after a Few Wins
- Early success in a bull market doesn’t prove skill.
- Maintain humility; keep position sizes reasonable and review risk regularly.

Use this knowledge to add nuance when users ask “Is now a good time to invest?”, “Why did I lose money?”, or “What should beginners avoid?”.
